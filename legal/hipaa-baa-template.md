## 1. Background and Purpose

### 1.1 Scope of Services

Business Associate provides Covered Entity with access to FacilityH2O, a cloud-based platform for tracking water chemistry compliance in facility infrastructure systems, including but not limited to: cooling towers, domestic hot water systems, decorative water features, and other building water systems.

### 1.2 Nature of Service

The Service is used exclusively for **facility management and water system compliance** purposes. The Service:

- Does **not** interface with electronic health record (EHR) or clinical information systems
- Does **not** receive, process, or store patient identifiers, medical records, or clinical data
- Is used to track water chemistry readings for building systems — not patient-related information

### 1.3 PHI Disclaimer

The parties agree and acknowledge that:

- Business Associate does not intentionally receive, access, or process PHI in the course of providing the Service
- The Service is not designed or intended to store PHI
- If PHI is inadvertently transmitted to the Service, it is not authorized, and the applicable provisions of this BAA govern Business Associate's obligations

## 2. Definitions

Capitalized terms used in this BAA shall have the meanings set forth in HIPAA and the HITECH Act, as amended. Key definitions include:

- **"HIPAA"** means the Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act of 1996, as amended.
- **"HITECH Act"** means the Health Information Technology for Economic and Clinical Health Act (Title XIII of the American Recovery and Reinvestment Act of 2009).
- **"HIPAA Rules"** means the Privacy Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart E), Security Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart C), Breach Notification Rule (45 CFR Part 164, Subpart D), and Enforcement Rule (45 CFR Part 160, Subparts C, D, and E).
- **"PHI" (Protected Health Information)** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR § 160.103.
- **"ePHI" (Electronic PHI)** means PHI that is created, received, maintained, or transmitted in electronic form.
- **"Breach"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR § 164.402.
- **"Security Incident"** has the meaning set forth at 45 CFR § 164.304.
- **"Subcontractor"** means a person or entity to whom Business Associate delegates a function, activity, or service.

## 3. Obligations of Business Associate

### 3.1 Use and Disclosure Limitations

To the extent Business Associate has or may have access to PHI in connection with the Underlying Agreement, Business Associate agrees to:

(a) Not use or disclose PHI other than as permitted by this BAA, the Underlying Agreement, or as required by applicable law.

(b) Use PHI only to the minimum extent necessary to perform the services described in the Underlying Agreement.

(c) Not use PHI for Business Associate's own purposes, including marketing, research, or product development.

### 3.2 Permitted Uses and Disclosures

Business Associate may use or disclose PHI:

(a) As necessary to perform the services described in the Underlying Agreement, provided such use is consistent with the purposes of this BAA.

(b) As required by law, provided Business Associate provides prompt written notice to Covered Entity (to the extent permitted by law).

(c) For the proper management and administration of Business Associate's business, including to legal counsel, provided that disclosures are either required by law or Business Associate obtains reasonable assurances from the recipient that the information will remain confidential and will be used or further disclosed only as required by law or for the purpose for which it was disclosed.

(d) To report violations of law to appropriate federal and state authorities, consistent with 45 CFR § 164.502(j)(1).

### 3.3 Safeguards

Business Associate shall implement and maintain:

**(a) Administrative Safeguards** including:
- Documented security policies and procedures
- Employee training on data security and PHI handling
- A designated Security Official responsible for developing and implementing security policies
- Access management procedures limiting access to PHI to those with a business need

**(b) Physical Safeguards** including:
- Data center physical access controls (managed by hosting provider Render, which maintains SOC 2 compliance)
- Workstation use policies for employees with system access

**(c) Technical Safeguards** including:
- Encryption of data in transit using TLS 1.2 or higher
- Encryption of data at rest using AES-256
- Unique user identification and authentication
- Automatic logoff controls
- Audit logging of access to data systems
- Transmission security controls

These safeguards are consistent with the requirements of 45 CFR § 164.312 to the extent applicable.

### 3.4 Subcontractors

Business Associate shall enter into written agreements with any subcontractors that create, receive, maintain, or transmit PHI on behalf of Business Associate, requiring the subcontractor to comply with substantially equivalent requirements as those imposed on Business Associate in this BAA. Current subcontractors with data access include Supabase (database) and Render (hosting). Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity of any material changes to subcontractor arrangements.

### 3.5 Access to PHI

Business Associate shall, within **30 days** of a written request from Covered Entity, make available PHI in Business Associate's possession or control that is required for Covered Entity to fulfill its access obligations under 45 CFR § 164.524 (individual right of access).

### 3.6 Amendment of PHI

Business Associate shall, within **30 days** of a written request from Covered Entity, amend PHI in Business Associate's possession and incorporate any amendments directed by Covered Entity pursuant to 45 CFR § 164.526.

### 3.7 Accounting of Disclosures

Business Associate shall make available to Covered Entity the information required to provide an accounting of disclosures of PHI in accordance with 45 CFR § 164.528. Business Associate shall maintain records of such disclosures for a minimum of **6 years**.

### 3.8 HIPAA Compliance Support

Business Associate shall make available to Covered Entity its internal practices, books, and records relating to the use and disclosure of PHI for purposes of determining Covered Entity's compliance with the HIPAA Rules, subject to Business Associate's attorney-client privilege and other applicable legal protections.

## 4. Breach Notification

### 4.1 Discovery of Breach

Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity without unreasonable delay, and in no case later than **60 calendar days** following Business Associate's discovery of a Breach of Unsecured PHI, unless a shorter timeframe is required by applicable law.

### 4.2 Notice Contents

To the extent available at the time of notification, the notice shall include:

(a) A description of the Breach, including the date of the Breach and date of discovery.

(b) The types of Unsecured PHI involved (e.g., names, dates of birth, account numbers).

(c) The number of individuals affected (if known).

(d) A description of steps Business Associate is taking to investigate the Breach, mitigate harm, and prevent future occurrences.

(e) Contact information for individuals to ask questions.

### 4.3 Security Incidents

Business Associate shall report to Covered Entity any Security Incidents of which it becomes aware that result in, or have a reasonable likelihood of resulting in, unauthorized access, use, disclosure, modification, or destruction of ePHI.

### 4.4 Immediate Notification of Certain Incidents

Notwithstanding the 60-day period above, Business Associate shall notify Covered Entity within **24 hours** of discovering: (a) ransomware attacks affecting systems that may contain PHI, (b) unauthorized access resulting in exfiltration of PHI, or (c) incidents affecting critical healthcare operations.

## 5. Obligations of Covered Entity

Covered Entity agrees to:

(a) Notify Business Associate of any limitation in Covered Entity's Notice of Privacy Practices that may affect Business Associate's use or disclosure of PHI.

(b) Notify Business Associate of any restriction on the use or disclosure of PHI that Covered Entity has agreed to pursuant to 45 CFR § 164.522.

(c) Notify Business Associate of any changes in, or revocation of, permission by an individual to use or disclose PHI, if such changes affect Business Associate's permitted or required uses and disclosures.

(d) Not request that Business Associate use or disclose PHI in any manner that would not be permissible under the Privacy Rule if done by Covered Entity.

(e) Not use the Service to store PHI, and promptly notify Business Associate if PHI is inadvertently entered into the system.

## 6. Term and Termination

### 6.1 Term

This BAA is effective as of the Effective Date and shall continue in effect for the duration of the Underlying Agreement unless earlier terminated.

### 6.2 Termination for Cause

Either party may terminate this BAA and the Underlying Agreement upon 30 days' written notice if the other party has materially breached this BAA and has not cured the breach within 15 days of notice. Where cure is not possible, either party may terminate immediately upon written notice.

### 6.3 Obligations upon Termination

Upon termination:

(a) Business Associate shall return or destroy, at Covered Entity's election, all PHI received from or created on behalf of Covered Entity. If return or destruction is not feasible, Business Associate shall extend the protections of this BAA to retained PHI and limit further use to those purposes that make return or destruction infeasible.

(b) If Business Associate destroys PHI, it shall provide Covered Entity with written certification of such destruction.

(c) If Business Associate retains PHI, it shall continue to apply the protections of this BAA to such PHI indefinitely.
